When schedules and attendance live in different places

Many small businesses still track staff schedules, late arrivals, absences, and attendance reports by hand. Common tools include WhatsApp groups, paper sheets, spreadsheets, or memory. That works until someone asks: “Who was supposed to be here Tuesday night?” or “Can we show attendance for payroll?”

The day-to-day cost

Mixed messages, missed shifts, and unclear records take time to fix. Owners and managers end up chasing details instead of running the floor.

What you actually need

A simple way to plan shifts, record clock-in and clock-out with a ZKTeco attendance system, and pull reports you can use—built around how your team really works.

Common questions

Short answers so you know what this is—and what it is not—before you reach out.

Do we need to already own a ZKTeco device?

Not necessarily. If you already have compatible hardware, we configure it. If you are starting fresh, we help you pick an appropriate ZKTeco model for your staff count and site.

How long does setup usually take?

It depends on staff count, locations, and reporting depth. After a short discovery call, we give you a realistic timeline—often a small team is faster than you might expect once the device and roster rules are clear.

Is this priced like a monthly SaaS subscription?

Pricing is based on your setup scope, not a generic per-seat table on this page. Many clients prefer a clear project-style quote for device work, configuration, and training.

Will this replace our full HR system?

No. This stays focused on roster and attendance operations. If you already use HR tools for hiring or benefits, this can sit beside them without trying to replace them.

Do you run payroll for us?

We do not process payroll or file taxes. We help you get clean attendance data and roster and attendance system exports your payroll person or accountant can use.

What if we have more than one location?

Multi-site is supported as an add-on when you need separate clocks or reports by site. We map that out in the discovery step so exports stay simple.
